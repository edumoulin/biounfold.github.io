**Short version — ADME and the Biological Time Dimension**

Binding is often the focus of early drug discovery. But once a molecule enters a biological system, what matters is not only whether it binds, but how exposure unfolds over time.

ADME — absorption, distribution, metabolism, and excretion — turns molecules from static objects into dynamic interventions. Duration, peaks, and persistence determine which biological regimes a drug actually occupies.

In this week’s BioUnfold, I explore why ADME becomes truly meaningful only once biology and mechanism are anchored — and why exposure is best understood as a problem of biological time.
