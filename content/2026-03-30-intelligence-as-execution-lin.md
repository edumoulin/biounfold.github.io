A molecule can generate revenue, but its value does not compound.

Growth in biotech comes from something else: the ability to generate the next asset.

That ability depends on how well an organization turns data and reasoning into execution.

Today, generating hypotheses is becoming easier. Execution is not.

Models can explore large spaces of possibilities, but experiments still require time, capital, and attention. The constraint is no longer generating ideas. It is deciding which ideas to act on.

This creates an execution boundary.

Within that boundary, companies capture full value by advancing their highest-confidence opportunities. Beyond it lies latent value — opportunities that only become transferable after enough execution establishes credibility.

The organizations that compound value most effectively are the ones where each experiment improves what gets done next.
